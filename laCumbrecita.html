Welcome to La Cumbrecita - welcome to Octoberfest!


Well, what's this? I thought we were in Argentina. Why are they coming back with Bavarian culture and Oktoberfest again? Are you serious?


The Argentinian town of La Cumbrecita was founded in 1934 by German immigrants. All street signs are in German, and a giant cuckoo clock greets visitors upon entering the town. The houses and food are reminiscent of Bavarian culture, and Oktoberfest is celebrated here as well. Tourists come from all over Argentina to experience this bizarre vision of a miniature Germany. Here, they can enjoy apple strudel in the caf√© and purchase small trinkets in the gift shops.
The locals live in wooden houses, some of which speak German. Great importance is placed on living in harmony with nature. This makes La Cumbrecita one of the most sustainable towns in the country. All of its electricity is generated from renewable energy. In addition to treating water for reuse, materials such as plastics, glass, and metals are recycled, and organic waste is turned into garden compost.
