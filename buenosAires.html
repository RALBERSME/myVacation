Buenos Aires - the capital of Argentina

The name Buenos Aires means "good air" or "good breezes" in Spanish. The city was founded in 1536 by Spanish sailors and was originally called "Santa María del Buen Ayre." The city was founded in 1536 by the Spanish sailor Pedro de Mendoza. Today, it has a population of approximately 3,120,000. It lies on the Río de la Plata, a funnel-shaped estuary of the Río Paraná and Río Uruguay. Buenos Aires is located in the subtropical climate zone. (hover over images to change size)

Argentina has an area of ​​2,780,400 km², making it the 8th largest country in the world. Argentina has a population of approximately 47.3 million. Its neighbors are Chile, Uruguay, Paraguay, Bolivia, and Brazil. Argentina's national tree is the coral tree. Two-thirds of the population are Catholic. 90% of Argentinians have European roots, and over half a million Italians live here.

The Argentinian flag was adopted in 1812. The colors celeste and white represent the blue sky with white clouds. The sun symbol in the center shows the sun of May. It is the symbol of the Incas and represents the sun god Inti. May also stands for the May Revolution of 1810, which marked the beginning of the independence movement from the Spanish Empire.
