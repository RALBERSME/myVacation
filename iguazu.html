The Iguazú Falls consist of 20 major and 255 minor waterfalls, stretching over a distance of 2.7 kilometers. Some are up to 82 meters high, while the majority are 64 meters high. Since most of the falls are located in Argentina, the greater panoramic view is available from the Brazilian side. The main waterfall (or waterfall system), colloquially known as the Garganta del Diablo or "Devil's Throat," is a U-shaped gorge 150 meters wide and 700 meters long.

The Iguazú Falls (Cataratas del Iguazú) are the waterfalls of the Iguazú River on the border between the Brazilian state of Paraná and the Argentine province of Misiones.
